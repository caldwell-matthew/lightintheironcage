PROOFREAD 

Scrupulosity, also known as 'religious OCD', or 'the doubting disease' is a thorn I am unfortunately afflicted with. It is a very painful disorder - not only is it mentally exhausting, it is doubly painful due to the fact it is nearly impossible to explain to someone why it is so bothersome. As Lewis said, "Mental pain is less dramatic than physical pain, but it is more common and also more hard to bear". Very much so with any kind of mental health struggle, but especially the case with obsessive-compulsive-disorders. 

Psalm 138:7-139:24 has been a balm to my soul during times of obsessive panic. I will obviously not post the entire section here, but I would like to highlight some of my favorite exerpts that have especially comforted me and I pray that maybe they can help you too. Like many Psalms, I like to treat it as a prayer and pray (sometimes even out loud) as I read while I deeply let the Words imprint on my soul. God's Word is a gift and a very comforting one, for it tells us whom God is - utterly kind and gentle in heart, everlasting and all knowing, mighty to save and deliver from sin and sorrow. Psalms 94:19 says that we anxious-riddled people need His consultations more than anything - "When my anxious thoughts multiply within me, Your consolations delight my soul." (NASB) Let us do so.  

> "Though I walk in the midst of trouble, you preserve my life; you stretch out your hand against the wrath of my enemies, and your right hand delivers me."  
> -- Psalms 138:7
